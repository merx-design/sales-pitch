## Section 1 — The problem we solve

### Headline
**Most platform projects fail before they start.**

### Body
You know the pattern. The agency quote comes back at six months and twice the budget you expected. The proposal is built around hours, not outcomes. Halfway through, scope drift starts. By launch, your team is dependent on the agency for every change, and a year later you're paying retainer fees just to update a hero image.

The market gives you two options that both fail you. Big agencies move slowly and price for hours that shouldn't be needed. Cheap shops cut corners and leave you with technical debt. There's a missing middle: serious delivery, fast, at a price that respects both sides.

## Section 2 — How we're different

### Headline
**We sell scope, not hours.**

### Body
Every Veren engagement commits to scope, timeline, and quality in writing. When we deliver faster, that's our margin. When we deliver slower, that's our loss. You know what you're paying for. You know when you'll have it.

This is possible because of how we deliver:

**A blueprint, not a blank page.** We don't build every project from zero. Our productized foundation absorbs the structural work — design system, page builder, component library, deployment pipeline — so we customize for you on top of something proven, not from scratch.

**Designer-led production.** Once the system is in place, pages are produced by designers and content operations, not gated by developer queues. Your platform scales with your needs, not our team's bandwidth.

**Senior judgment throughout.** Architecture, design direction, and quality review are handled by founders, not delegated to junior staff billed at senior rates. You're paying for the experience that matters.

**A 72-hour rhythm.** Updates every three days. Three formal approval gates across the engagement, not fifteen. You stay informed without being asked to micromanage.

## Section 6 — How a project actually runs

### Headline
**Eight weeks, in detail.**

### Body
Here's what a Foundation engagement (the middle tier) looks like end to end:

**Week 0 — Discovery.** Two days of structured workshops. Stakeholder mapping. Technical architecture. Content readiness. Risk register. You leave with a locked scope and a decision about whether to proceed.

**Weeks 1-2 — Foundation build.** Design system, page builder, content model, integrations. End of week 2: system approval gate. Once approved, the system is locked.

**Weeks 3-5 — Production ramp.** Pages designed and built on the locked system. Integration logic finalized. Production sample review at the midpoint.

**Weeks 6-7 — Production run and pre-launch.** Bulk page production. Content migration. QA. Performance testing. Final pre-launch review.

**Week 8 — Launch and stabilization.** Platform goes live. One-week active monitoring. Training sessions for your team. Documentation handover.

**Week 9 onward — Independence.** Your team operates the platform. We're available if you want us. We're not necessary if you don't.

This isn't an aspirational timeline. This is the rhythm the model is built to produce.

## Section 7 — Frequently asked questions

### Headline
**Honest answers to honest questions.**

### Body

**How can you commit to fixed timelines when most agencies can't?**
Two reasons. First, our blueprint absorbs the structural work that's normally rebuilt from scratch on every project. Second, we run paid discovery before committing — which means by the time we sign for delivery, both sides have agreed on scope, preconditions, and risks. Discovery is our qualification filter as much as our scoping tool. Projects that can't be committed to don't get committed to.

**What if I need changes after launch?**
Your team is trained to make most changes themselves — that's what the page builder and component system enable. For larger changes, we offer optional retainers or scoped change-order engagements. The retainer is genuinely optional. Many clients use us once a year for an evolution; some don't need us at all.

**Why Sanity instead of WordPress / Webflow / HubSpot CMS?**
Sanity is the most mature content platform for the kind of work we do — structured content, multi-language, editorial workflows, integration-heavy. It's where the serious B2B and commerce projects live in our segment. We use other platforms when they're the right answer, but for the work we specialize in, Sanity is the strongest foundation.

**Where is your team located?**
Engineering in Romania. Client leadership in Germany. Both are full members of the firm. Our buyers are in DACH; our engineering hub is in one of Europe's strongest tech talent markets. The combination is what makes our cost structure work without compromising on quality.

**What if discovery shows we're not the right fit?**
You keep the deliverables — scope, architecture brief, risk register, recommendations. They're useful regardless of who you work with next. Discovery is paid because the deliverables are valuable, not because we're holding them hostage.

**Are you replacing a traditional agency relationship, or working alongside one?**
Either, depending on what you need. Some clients bring us in as their primary platform partner. Some bring us in alongside an existing agency that handles brand, paid media, or content production. We're clear about what we do and what we don't do, and we work well in both setups.
