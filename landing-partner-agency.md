## Section 3 — How the partnership works

### Headline
**Three ways we can structure this.**

### Body
The right structure depends on your agency's positioning, your client base, and how visible you want us to be. We're flexible on this — what matters is that the structure works for both sides commercially and operationally.

**White-label delivery.** We deliver under your brand. Your client never sees Veren. We're invisible delivery infrastructure. Best when your agency has strong brand equity you want to protect or extend.

**Co-branded delivery.** "Agency + Veren" framing. Your client sees both names. Best when your agency wants to signal specialist capability without claiming to do the platform work in-house.

**Referral with direct contracting.** You refer the client; we contract directly. You take a referral commission. Best when the engagement is significantly outside your agency's normal scope, or when the client specifically wants a direct relationship with the platform partner.

We can talk through which structure fits your agency in the partnership conversation.

## Section 6 — What kind of agencies we partner with

### Headline
**Honest about fit.**

### Body
We partner well with agencies that:

- Have a base of mid-market clients (typically 10M+ in revenue) where platform conversations come up regularly
- Have a strong client-facing team but limited or stretched delivery capacity for complex platform work
- Specialize in HubSpot, RevOps, demand generation, content marketing, or related services where the website is part of the stack but not the core deliverable
- Want a long-term partner relationship, not a one-off subcontracting arrangement

We're probably not the right partner for:

- Agencies that compete primarily on price for simple website work (our cost structure is built for serious platform engagements, not commodity sites)
- Agencies that already have strong in-house Sanity or headless platform capability (you don't need us)
- Agencies looking for a white-label developer to dispatch tickets to (we operate as a strategic partner, not a ticket queue)

The partnership conversation will tell us both whether we're a fit. If we're not, we'll say so directly.

## Section 8 — Frequently asked questions

### Headline
**The questions agency partners actually ask.**

### Body

**Are you going to steal our clients?**
No. The partnership models are structured so we cannot — operationally and contractually. Clients who come through your agency stay your clients. We're delivery infrastructure, not a competitive sales channel. The partner agreement makes this explicit.

**Can we talk to your existing partner agency for a reference?**
Once a partnership conversation is serious, yes. We'll arrange the introduction with our existing partner's permission. We don't broker reference calls speculatively because our partners' time is limited and they prefer talking to agencies that have a real interest in moving forward.

**What's your capacity? Can you handle multiple projects from us?**
Honest answer: it depends on tier and timing. We have capacity for a Platform engagement plus several Foundations or Starters at any given moment. Larger volume requires planning together — we'll tell you what's possible 4-8 weeks ahead so you can pace your pipeline accordingly.

**How do we handle pricing on the client side?**
Several models work. We can give you our rate card and you mark up. We can quote together with a revenue split on the project. We can work to a target client price and reverse-engineer the engagement. We're flexible on commercial structure as long as the result is sustainable for both sides.

**What if a project goes wrong?**
We commit to scope and timeline in writing, with the partner agency and the end client. Delays caused by us extend at our cost; delays caused by the client extend at theirs (per the contract). If something genuinely goes wrong, we resolve it together — not by hiding from the agency or the client. Partner trust is the only thing that lets this model work, and we treat it accordingly.

**Are you a replacement for our existing dev team or just an extension?**
Depends on what you have. Some agencies use us as their entire platform delivery capability. Some use us only for Sanity-specific projects and keep their existing team for other work. We don't require exclusivity — we want to be the right partner for the work we're best at, not the only partner you have.
